View live tracker →

What I Built

  • Pulls a full year of $MU (Micron) stock price history and calculates the 9, 21, and 48-day Exponential Moving Averages (EMAs).
  • Automatically updates the price history, rebuilds the chart, and emails it to me after market close every 24 hours — so I get the update even when I don't have my laptop open.
  • Automatically flags EMA crossovers — when short-term and long-term trends cross — and emails a daily chart with a summary of how today's price action differs from yesterday's.

Thesis

Based on a theory from a YouTuber I follow who observed that $MU was tracking the 9 and 21-day EMAs closely, with strong support at the 48-day. I wanted to plot and test it myself over several weeks rather than take someone else's word for it.

Findings matched: since May 2026, every time $MU retraces below the 9-day EMA but stays above the 21-day, it subsequently rallies — creating a consistent dip-buying signal.

Next Steps

Repeat the same analysis for the remaining stocks in my personal portfolio to test whether the EMA pattern is unique to Micron (as a sector leader during a hot AI hardware cycle) or a more generalizable signal.
