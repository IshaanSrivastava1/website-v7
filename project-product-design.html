Product Design — MIE540 Design Project

PFD · FMEA · Taguchi Methods · AutoCAD · 3D Printing · Jan 2025 – Apr 2025

What I Did

  • Created a comprehensive business case for a sample ping-pong launcher, including a Product Functional Decomposition (PFD) diagram and Failure Mode Effect Analysis (FMEA) table to identify and prioritize critical design parameters.
  • Used Taguchi experiments with an L9 orthogonal matrix to systematically determine how sensitive the product was to noise factors — a structured way to optimize design robustness with minimal test runs.
  • Developed a supplier scorecard system to evaluate vendors based on cost, quality, and delivery metrics.
  • Created a functioning 3D-printed prototype using AutoCAD to model the parts before fabrication.

Outcomes

This project bridged engineering and business: the Taguchi approach gave a statistically grounded way to select design parameters, while the supplier scorecard introduced real procurement thinking into the design phase. The combination mirrors how industrial product development teams operate — design and sourcing working together from the start.
